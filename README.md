# Patina

> Time reveals value.

Patina is a price intelligence app for collectible assets — cars, bikes, watches, and beyond. Think Bloomberg Terminal for people who invest in beautiful things.

---

## The idea

I've always been drawn to things that age well. Old machines. Objects with history. The kind of stuff that quietly becomes more valuable the longer it exists.

But when I started looking at how people actually buy and sell collectible cars and bikes in India, I found something broken, there's no clean, data-driven way to know if an asset is at its peak, in a dip, or mid-climb. Collectors guess. They call dealers who want to sell them something. They post in WhatsApp groups and get ten different opinions. They trust their gut on decisions worth lakhs.

Every other asset class has intelligence tools. Equities have Bloomberg. Real estate has PropTech. Collectibles have nothing.

That's the gap Patina fills.

---

## What it does

Tracks appreciation and depreciation trends for exotic and collectible assets. Shows you whether something is a good buy right now, and where the price might go. Starts with cars and bikes, expands into watches, art, and beyond.

---

## What I'm building toward

I want Patina to become the default place serious collectors go before making any significant purchase. Not just a pretty chart, a trusted opinion. The kind of tool that changes how a decision gets made.

Long term, I want to map the relationships between collectible asset classes that nobody has studied at a data level. Do vintage Indian bikes move after international auction results? Does a strong Sotheby's season predict what happens to the Indian exotic car market six months later? That's the question I want Patina to eventually answer.
